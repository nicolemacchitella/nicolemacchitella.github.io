Money Illusion in Large Language Models: An Exploratory Replication Study

With Milos Ciganovic and Luca Panaccione · Journal of Behavioral Economics for Policy, Vol. 9(1), pp. 75–80, 2025

We replicate the classic money illusion experiments of Shafir, Diamond, and Tversky (1997) across six large language models. Our exploratory evidence suggests that LLMs generally reason in real rather than nominal terms for objective evaluations, but align more closely with human responses when subjective or social judgements are involved.
