Research

My research interests lie in industrial organization, digital markets, and innovation economics. I am particularly interested in competition economics — including the regulation of digital platforms, excessive pricing, and the intersection of competition policy with technological change.

2025

Money Illusion in Large Language Models: An Exploratory Replication Study

With Milos Ciganovic and Luca Panaccione. We replicate the classic money illusion experiments of Shafir, Diamond, and Tversky (1997) across six large language models. Our exploratory evidence suggests that LLMs generally reason in real rather than nominal terms for objective evaluations, but align more closely with human responses — including susceptibility to money illusion — when subjective or social judgements are involved.

Journal of Behavioral Economics for Policy, Vol. 9, Issue 1, pp. 75–80.
